Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about E-Pharma.

E-Pharma provides centralized inventory management that synchronizes stock levels across all pharmacy outlets in real-time. The system tracks inventory at each location while maintaining a consolidated view for the entire chain. It supports automatic stock transfers between branches, centralized purchasing, location-specific pricing, and provides alerts for low stock levels at any outlet. You can view and manage inventory across all locations from a single dashboard.
E-Pharma seamlessly integrates with HIMS to receive electronic prescriptions directly from consulting doctors. When a doctor prescribes medication, it's automatically transmitted to the pharmacy system and converted into dispensing units without manual intervention. The system validates drug interactions, checks patient allergies, applies FIFO/LIFO rules for dispensing, and automatically updates inventory. Pharmacists receive immediate notifications of new prescriptions and can process them efficiently.
Yes, E-Pharma includes comprehensive controlled substance tracking with specialized workflows for Schedule II-V medications. The system maintains detailed logs of all controlled substance transactions, requires additional authorization for dispensing, tracks prescriber DEA numbers, monitors prescription quantities and refills, generates required regulatory reports, and provides complete audit trails. It's fully compliant with GCC health authority regulations including NABIDH, Malaffi, and electronic claims submission standards.
Absolutely. E-Pharma supports multi-mode billing including cash, insurance, and corporate accounts. The system includes real-time insurance eligibility verification, automatic benefit calculation, electronic claims submission (e-claims), claims tracking and reconciliation, automatic payment posting, and detailed reimbursement reports. It integrates with major insurance providers in the GCC region and supports both pre-authorization and direct billing workflows.
E-Pharma provides advanced batch and expiry control with automated alerts for medications approaching expiration. The system tracks batch numbers, manufacturing dates, and expiry dates for all inventory. It automatically implements FIFO (First In First Out) or LIFO (Last In First Out) dispensing based on your preference, generates expiry reports, sends alerts for near-expiry items, and prevents dispensing of expired medications. The barcode integration ensures accurate batch tracking from receiving to dispensing.
